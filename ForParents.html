  • For Parents

In this activity, you can help youths diagnosed with autism interact more deeply with Money & Me by showing how skills learned from the web app can be used in daily life. Since some people on the spectrum have difficulty relating digital objects (such as items seen on the screen) to the physical world, you can bring further enrichment to their experience with this web app by working together with the youth to follow the game’s actions and simulating the shopping and money handling experience.


For this activity, you will need:
  • Money -- choose how much you want and what you want to use (bills or coins). Make sure you have enough!
  • Shopping items -- you can use food items, office supplies, or whatever you want!
  • Price stickers -- you can get paper and write prices on them, then use tape to stick to items.
  • Shopping bag/basket (optional) -- used to carry items once they are selected for purchase.
  • Shopping list (optional) -- used to help your child select the items they need.

    • 1. Make sure your child has enough money to pay and you have enough for change. Tell them how much money they have and that they can spend only that much or less. Tell them what to buy. Guide them through choosing the items if they can’t figure out what they should be looking for. Whenever they select an item, tell them how much the item costs and how much money they would have remaining if they buy it.
